Visit 1 — Initial Consultation

28 February 2026 · Dr. Life Chiropractic

🔍 What We Found

After reviewing your history and performing a thorough examination, we identified several key issues working together to cause your symptoms:

  • Cervical spine dysfunction — Fixations (stiff joints) at C2, C3–4, and T1–3. Muscle spasm in the upper cervical and suboccipital muscles. This is likely driving your headaches.
  • Cervicogenic headache pattern — Your headaches start at the base of your skull and are linked to neck stiffness. Your HIT-6 score (63/78) confirms severe impact. Research strongly supports spinal manipulation for this type of headache.
  • Lumbar restriction — Fixations at L3–L5 and S1 with reduced extension and flexion. Consistent with prolonged sitting posture and your fingertip-to-floor test (22cm).
  • Postural decompensation — Forward head carriage and increased thoracic curve. This is common with desk work and significantly loads the neck (every inch of forward head posture adds roughly 10 lbs of strain to neck muscles).
  • Unresolved whiplash (2019) — Your previous car accident injury was never properly rehabilitated. The residual instability and compensation patterns are likely contributing to your current symptoms.
  • Balance and flexibility deficits — Single-leg balance (18–22s) and fingertip-to-floor (22cm) both below expected. These indicate core stability and posterior chain issues that maintain the mechanical dysfunction.
⚡ What We Did
  • Comprehensive history and physical examination
  • 8 vital health indicator tests (baseline established)
  • Cervical and lumbar range of motion assessment
  • Full spine palpation and motion analysis
  • Orthopaedic testing (Spurling's, cervical distraction, SLR, FABER, Kemp's)
  • Validated outcome questionnaires: NDI, HIT-6, ODI (baseline scores recorded)
  • Postural analysis
📋 Your Care Plan

Based on your examination findings, here's what we recommend:

  • Phase 1 — Relief (Weeks 1–4): 2–3 visits per week. Focus on reducing pain, improving cervical and lumbar mobility, and decreasing headache frequency. Gentle adjustments (we'll use low-force techniques since you're nervous about cracking — there are many effective gentle methods).
  • Phase 2 — Corrective (Weeks 5–10): 1–2 visits per week. Strengthening exercises, posture retraining, and addressing the underlying dysfunction. We'll start working on your balance and flexibility scores.
  • Phase 3 — Maintenance (Ongoing): Periodic check-ins. Ergonomic adjustments for your desk setup. Continued home exercises.

Expected outcomes: Based on the research and your presentation, we expect meaningful improvement in headache frequency within 2–3 weeks, and your NDI/ODI scores should show clinically significant improvement by your 6-week re-assessment.

🏠 What To Do At Home
  • Chin tucks — 10 reps, 3 times daily (corrects forward head posture)
  • Cat-cow stretches — 10 reps, morning and evening (mobilises thoracic and lumbar spine)
  • Doorway chest stretch — 30 seconds each side, twice daily (opens chest, counters desk posture)
  • Heat pack on neck/upper back — 15 minutes in the evening
  • Sleep position — Try to transition from stomach to side sleeping with a supportive pillow. Stomach sleeping significantly loads the cervical spine.
  • Desk setup — Screen at eye level, elbows at 90°, feet flat. Take a 2-minute movement break every 45 minutes.
